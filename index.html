What Can Everyone Do?

Buy meals for health care workers from your favorite local restaurants.

Help your favorite local restaurant stay in business during the crisis by giving them business.

Support and show your appreciation to health care workers who are working literally around the clock.

Every meal you buy gets delivered daily to local hospitals.

Restaurants

Restaurants everywhere are facing an unprecedented crisis.

Many owners are independent, small operations that can not withstand sustained sales declines.

Small, independent, family-owned and operated restaurants are a critical part of the social fabric.

They feed us, nurture us, and care for us; on weekends and on holidays, year in and year out. They provide gathering places for friends and family and contribute to the rich diversity of our communities.

They have always supported us. Now let us support them.

Medical Professionals

Critical health care infrastructure in many areas is under tremendous strain.

Retired medical professionals are being asked to come out of retirement. Part-timers are being urged to be full time.

Doctors, nurses, hospital workers, etc. are working around the clock, putting their own health and well-being in danger, to save the lives of others.

They work long hours to save lives. Let us keep them nourished.

Donations

Several donors have pledged to match meals to be gifted to frontline health care workers.

When matching donations are available, for every meal that is purchased for a health care worker, an additional one will be donated.

If you are able to contribute in this way, please get in touch directly with eric@shelter-in-plates.com.

About

This site and service was created and is being maintained entirely by volunteers.

We believe that local, independent restaurants are the lifeblood of our communities.

We recognize the incredible sacrifice and selfless gesture of medical professionals in this time of need.

Also, selfishly, we would like to emerge from this pandemic with the option to eat somewhere other than Red Lobster.

Contributors

Inspiration

This project was inspired by amazing Joanna Argus Kirkendall of The Pleasantry and 1215 Wine Bar & Coffee Lab in beautiful Cincinnati.

Joanna rallied her community to donate over 3000 meals in a few short days to Cincinnati area hospitals.

We're hoping to encourage other communities to support both their front line health care workers and their independent local restaurants by doing the same.
